The Tech-Board Story

(Stranger than fiction)
The true story of the most dramatic "Greenfield Startup" failure in British manufacturing history.

Welcome

At the time it was thought to be the largest venture-capital backed Greenfield manufacturing startup in British History. In 1993 one man raised over £35 million from the City of London to build a new manufacturing plant in Wales. Over the next 8 years the plant was built, started production, went into receivership three times, and finally was shut down for the final time.

We lived through the madness, and thought that we should commit our memories to a web page, for posterity.

Ongoing Sagas

From sudden high-volume time checks ("WHAT'S THE TIME?") to the legendary Malaysian nightie export legal dispute, the London setup laid down a colorful blueprint for what was to follow.

Enterprise House (Rassau)

A couple of weeks after the Deal was signed, the team started commuting to Wales. The Mill was to be built on the Rassau Industrial Estate, near Ebbw Vale. Temporary space was secured at Enterprise House.

"In the original business plan, there was a lot written about employing modern management practices – a 'single status' company. Then the office furniture arrived: a really big desk for Malcolm, a big one for Tony, and smaller ones for everyone else. This was a defining moment."

Into Operation

Following the opening, a stringent 24-hour baseline test was required by the financing banks. Miraculously, the persistent structural board wave disappeared exactly for the 24-hour baseline performance trial window, unlocking the capital release sequence perfectly before immediately reverting right back to a wavy production standard post-evaluation.

...And The Gates Close

One afternoon, everyone was called out to the car park. A list of names was read out to stand aside; the remainder were informed that the operation had suddenly collapsed into immediate receivership over a single banking cycle.
